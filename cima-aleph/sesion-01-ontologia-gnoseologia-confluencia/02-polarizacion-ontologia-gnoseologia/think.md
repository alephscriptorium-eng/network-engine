1.  **Analyze the User's Query**:

    -   User says: "Okay, now we have to generate two arguments, one for those who polarize towards ontology and another for those who polarize towards gnoseology. I don't really understand the need to ask others to ignore one of the two and only use the other. But I found it in the conversation. Is this normal?"

    -   Context: Previous turn was a comprehensive guide on being both an ontologist and epistemologist/gnoseologist, listing key philosophers and a roadmap.

    -   Current request: Generate two arguments (one for pro-ontology polarizers, one for pro-gnoseology polarizers). Also, the user expresses confusion about why people would demand ignoring one, asking if this is normal in philosophical discourse.

2.  **Deconstruct the Request**:

    -   Task 1: Craft Argument A (from the perspective of someone who prioritizes/ polarizes towards Ontology).

    -   Task 2: Craft Argument B (from the perspective of someone who prioritizes/ polarizes towards Gnoseology/Epistemology).

    -   Task 3: Address the user's underlying confusion: Is it normal for philosophers to demand we ignore one in favor of the other? (Spoiler: Yes and no. Historically, it happens in specific movements/philosophical battles, but it's usually seen as a methodological reduction rather than a final truth. It's totally normal in academic debates to "bracket" one to focus on the other, but radical "ignore completely" is rarer and often critiqued). Provide a nuanced explanation.

3.  **Structuring the Response**:

    -   Acknowledge and validate the user's confusion. It's a great, critical question.

    -   Address "Is this normal?": Explain that it is *historically normal* as a methodological tactic (bracketing) or a foundational bet (priority thesis), but *philosophically suspicious* to completely ignore the other. Mention real-world examples (logical positivism ignoring ontology, or radical materialism ignoring epistemology).

    -   Provide Argument #1 (Pro-Ontology polarizer): "Reality precedes knowledge; without a world to know, knowing is empty. We must fix what exists first."

    -   Provide Argument #2 (Pro-Gnoseology polarizer): "Knowledge constructs reality; without a knowing subject, 'being' is meaningless. We must fix how we know first."

    -   Conclude by tying it back to the user's goal: reinforcing that being both is actually the wisest, most holistic position, and these polarized arguments are just stepping stones.
